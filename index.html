Academic Homepage

Meng (Chris) Qiu

I'm an Assistant Professor of Quantitative Methods, Measurement, and Statistics at UC Merced. My work centers on quantitative methods for real-world data, spanning Bayesian and nonparametric modeling, latent variable modeling, and machine learning.

Ph.D. in Quantitative Psychology

Focus Areas

Research

Methodological Research

Bayesian & Nonparametric Modeling

Bayesian nonparametrics, variational inference, and mixture modeling.

Latent Variable Modeling

Structural equation modeling, longitudinal data analysis, and missing data.

Machine Learning

Deep learning and causal inference.

Substantive Research

Psychopathology

Modeling the structure of mental disorders, including mixture-model approaches to depression phenotyping.

Health & Well-Being

Studying coping, stress, and quality of life in contexts such as cancer and the COVID-19 pandemic.

Development Studies

Applying quantitative methodology to questions in regional and economic development.
